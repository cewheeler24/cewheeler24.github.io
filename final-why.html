An Expanded User Base

A common argument against implementing accessibility features is that "it won't affect that many people." However, according to the World Health Organization, about 16% of the global population lives with a disability. Ensuring that digital content is accessible opens the content to a much wider audience than if accessibility is ignored.

Additionally, accessibility featurs improve the experience not just for those living with disabilities, but everyone. For example, if you broke your arm, you may not be able to use a mouse easily. If a website is designed with keyboard navigation, this is less of an issue for the user. The website would otherwise be inaccessible to you during that time.

If a user is in an environment where they cannot use their device's audio capabilities, captions on a video would not only aid someone who is hard of hearing, but anyone in that situation. Captions can also help users who are not fluent in the language being spoken, alloweing for deeper comprehension.
